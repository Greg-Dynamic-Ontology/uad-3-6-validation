Methodology

Ontology Test-Driven Development

Ontology Test-Driven Development (OTDD) is an engineering methodology for developing semantic systems through incremental discovery, executable verification, and complete traceability.

Rather than beginning with a complete ontology, OTDD derives semantic models from authoritative source material and continuously verifies their implementation.

Engineering Cycle

  1. Identify a semantic competency.
  2. Create representative examples.
  3. Observe the current implementation.
  4. Extend the ontology.
  5. Implement supporting software.
  6. Verify the competency.
  7. Record engineering evidence.
  8. Refactor while preserving meaning.

Guiding Principles

  • Derivation, not divination.
  • Meaning before implementation.
  • Traceability over ceremony.
  • Engineering knowledge belongs in the graph.
  • Human-readable artifacts are projections of semantic engineering knowledge.
